How Matched Betting Works

Matched betting is a way to turn free bookmaker offers into real, risk-free profit by betting on all outcomes of an event using two sites:

  • A bookmaker – where you place your free bet
  • A betting exchange – where you “lay” (bet against) the same outcome

Step-by-Step Example

  1. Sign up to a bookmaker offering “Bet £10, Get £30”
  2. Place a qualifying bet (e.g., £10 on Team A to win at the bookmaker)
  3. Lay that same bet on a betting exchange (Team A to NOT win)
  4. Once the match finishes, you break even — but now you get £30 in free bets
  5. Repeat with free bets, using the same back and lay process, to make a profit

Why It Works

You're covering all possible outcomes, so you don't rely on luck. The profit comes from the free bets, not gambling.

What You Need

  • An account with a bookmaker
  • An account with a betting exchange (like Betfair or Smarkets)
  • A small amount of starting cash to place bets and cover exchange liability

Note: Matched betting is legal and profits are tax-free in the UK.
